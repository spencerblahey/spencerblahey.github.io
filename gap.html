Gap Year

Whistler, BC - October 2021 to May 2022

Whistler Adaptive Sports Program (WASP)

Position: Mountain Instructor

The Whistler Adaptive team is focused on introducing individuals with disabilities to mountain sports. They break down the financial, physical and social barriers and provide them with the environment, support and opportunity to become physically active. We focus on abilities rather than disabilities.

This was one of the most rewarding experiences that I have had up to date. I received specialized training to coach through both physical and cognitive disabilities using equipment like; sit-skis, three-track kits, headsets and microphones (for visual impairments), tethers, etc. The individuals that I worked with were some of the most inspirational I have ever met. I formed lifelong connections with many, and truly began to understand the meaning of Universal Design. I will be forever grateful for the opportunity to have contributed to such an outstanding and unique team.

Taking a client for a sit-ski on 7th Heaven Chair.

The Adventure Group (TAG)

Position: Alpine Snowshoe Guide

As a secondary job to instructing, I decided to become a snowshoe guide and run alpine tours for The Adventure Group once a week. Coming from Northern Ontario, my background was tailored for the position. Growing up, I often planned snowmobiling/snowshoeing routes to new ice fishing lakes from their respective access roads using satellite imagery.

The position was incredible for improving both my communication and leadership skills, at times having to guide groups of up to 10 people. It also entailed the continuous assessment of my guests body language, ensuring they were fit to carry on with the challenges that the tour was presenting.

Running one of my favourite tours, "Natural Mystic".

Boston Bar, BC - May 2022 to August 2022

REO Rafting Ltd.

Position: White Water Raft Guide

After hearing incredible river expedition stories from several companions in Whistler, I set forward with a vision of also becoming a white water rafting guide. The thrill and adventure of the position, the technical skills it would leverage, and the leadership it entailed, all sounded up my alley.

I began as a trip assistant with minimal experience, helping to move boats, prepare equipment, and gear-up our customers. Overtime, I began to understand river hydrology, was trained as a Swiftwater Rescue Technician, and received my BCROA guiding certificate. This enabled me to guide over 50 safe trips by the end of the four months. I became a trip lead, responsible for the safety of our guests, organising the rafts, and producing operational reports. The role and the lifestyle we lived at camp will never be forgotten.

One of the most memorable trips of the season guiding.
